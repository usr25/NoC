The testing has been done mostly through cutechess (https://github.com/cutechess/cutechess)

The following command is to be used to properly test 2 engines:
./cutechess-cli -engine name="NoC NNUE" cmd="./NoCNNUE" arg="--nnue=/home/NNUE.nnue" -engine name="NoC Classic" cmd="./NoCClassic" -each tc=10+0.5 proto=uci -pgnout "output.pgn" -openings file="opening_suite.pgn" -ratinginterval 10 -sprt elo0=1 elo1=10 alpha=0.1 beta=0.1 -tb /SYZYGY_Folder -tbpieces 4 -resign movecount=10 score=1000 -games 2 -rounds 100 -maxmoves 300 -repeat 2 -concurrency 6

With the following being an example testing a version of the engine against itself without Gaviota tablebases. One version receives the NNUE as an argument while the other one does not.

./cutechess-cli -engine name="NoC NNUE" cmd="/home/j/Desktop/Chess/Engines/NoCNNUE" arg="--nnue=/home/j/Desktop/Chess/Engine/NNUE.nnue" -engine name="NoC Classic" cmd="/home/j/Desktop/Chess/Engines/NoCClassic" -each tc=10+0.5 proto=uci -pgnout "output.pgn" -openings file="/home/j/Desktop/Chess/NoC/opening_suite.pgn" -ratinginterval 10 -sprt elo0=1 elo1=10 alpha=0.1 beta=0.1 -tb /3-4-5_Syzygy/3-4-5 -tbpieces 4 -resign movecount=10 score=1000 -games 2 -rounds 100 -maxmoves 300 -repeat 2 -concurrency 6
